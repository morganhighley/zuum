Platform Drivers

A driver supporting the platform capabilities can advertise multiple managed subdevices underneath that driver. Platforms typically communicate with a gateway device or a cloud service to enumerate and control the attached devices.

Devices on a platform may be physical devices or virtual ones. For example, a service with lighting devices may also advertise room level control. In such cases, both the light and the room may be listed as managed devices.

The list of managed devices may change at any time due to new devices being paired to the system or being removed. At the time of writing, the actual discovery, pairing, and configuration of devices on the platform device is not covered in the capability definitions. The device’s user interface is used to perform those operations.

The managed devices of a platform may have their own configuration items. Applications must check the managed device’s status to determine if configuration is needed. Upon a restart of the driver, the application will likely need to configure the platform device first, wait for the managed device list to be updated, and then apply the managed device’s configuration.
